In era 1890

Fashion in the 1890s, part of the Late Victorian era, was elegant and modest. Women wore long dresses with very tight waists using corsets, high necklines, and large puffed sleeves. Hats, gloves, and detailed decorations were important accessories. Men wore formal suits, often three-piece, with high-collared shirts and hats like the top hat. Dark colors were common. Overall, fashion focused on sophistication, modesty, and showing social status.


In era 1920

Fashion in the 1920s was modern and elegant. Women wore shorter dresses with loose styles and simple designs. Flapper dresses were very popular. They also wore hats, gloves, and pearl necklaces. Short bob hairstyles became fashionable. Men wore suits with ties and hats. Dark colors were common. Overall, 1920s fashion showed freedom and a modern style.


In era 1950

Fashion in the 1950s was elegant and stylish. Women wore fitted dresses with full skirts and narrow waists. Gloves, handbags, and pearl necklaces were popular. Curled hairstyles became fashionable. Men wore suits, shirts, and ties. Jeans and leather jackets were also common. Overall, 1950s fashion showed elegance and classic style.
